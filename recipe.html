Tamales Verdes De Pollo (Green Salsa Tamales with Chicken)

Tamales first originated in Mesoamerica, the south-central part of Mexico in around 8,000 B.C.E. The Aztecs, Maya, and ancient Olmec and Toltec people all made a variety of ground corn dough stuffed with differents meats or vegetables. The reason they started to make tamales was because they were often traveling and they found tamales to be a good travel food due to the corn husk keeping the food inside fresh for longer as well as the tamale's easy accessbility to eat.

This recipe makes a delicious batch of green tamales which have a spicy yet flavorful taste. The taste of the tamales makes up for the long process you have to go through in order to make this popular dish!

Ingredients

  • Masa (Corn Dough)
  • Corn Husks
  • Green Tomatoes and Green Chiles
  • Chicken
  • Salt, Pepper, Garlic


Recipe Directions

  1. Boil your chicken for 1 hour (with an onion and garlic blended with water) whilst you prepare everything else.
  2. Boil your green tomatoes and green chiles for them to blend easier.
  3. With the chicken broth, knead the masa so it becomes a soft texture.
  4. Soak the corn husks in water.
  5. Once your green tomatoes and green chiles are boiled, blend them with a mix of salt, pepper, and garlic.
  6. Fry this same green sauce in oil to get a richer taste.
  7. After your chicken has boiled, shred it into smaller pieces and mix it with the green sauce.
  8. When your masa is kneaded enough, spread it along the inside of each corn husk.
  9. Place a well amount of sauced chicken inside each tamale you make, then fold them.
  10. Once you've folded your tamales, put them in a steamer with water for around 2-3 hours depending on how thoroughly you'd like them cooked.
  11. Once your tamale is cooked thoroughly, enjoy!
