Tamales Verdes De Pollo

This recipe makes a delicious batch of green tamales which have a spicy yet flavorful taste. The taste of the tamales makes up for the long process you have to go through in order to make this popular dish!

Tamales first originated in Mesoamerica, the south-central part of Mexico in around 8,000 B.C.E. The Aztecs, Maya, Olmec, and Toltec people all made a variety of ground corn dough stuffed with differents meats or vegetables. The reason they started to make tamales was because they were often traveling and they found tamales to be a good travel food due to the corn husk keeping the food inside fresh for longer as well as the tamale's easy accessbility to eat.

Ingredients
  • Masa (Corn Dough)
  • Corn Husks
  • Green Tomatoes
  • Green Chiles
  • Cilantro
  • Chicken
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic
Instructions
  • Boil your chicken (with an onion and garlic) whilst you prepare everything else. Afterwards, boil your green tomatoes and green chiles for them to blend easier. With the chicken broth, knead the masa so it becomes a soft texture.
  • Soak the corn husks in water, and blend your boiled green tomatoes and green chiles with a mix of cilantro, salt, pepper, and garlic. After your chicken has boiled, shred it into smaller pieces and mix it with the green sauce.
  • When your masa is kneaded enough, spread it along the inside of each corn husk.
  • Place a well amount of sauced chicken inside each tamale you make, then fold them.
  • Once you've folded your tamales, put them in a steamer with water for around 2-3 hours depending on how thoroughly you'd like them cooked, then enjoy!
